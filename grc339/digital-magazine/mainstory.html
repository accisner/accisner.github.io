Cast Iron Veggies with Garlic, Lemon, Crushed Red Pepper, and Mint Leaves

Get ready to "sear"iously elevate your culinary game with our Cast Iron Seared Zucchini recipe. This dish is "pan-tastic," packed with flavors, and guaranteed to make your taste buds "sizzle" with delight. But here's the twist ‚Äì it's not just about zucchinis; this cooking method is "veg-tastic" and transferable to a variety of vegetable dishes. So, whether you're a "zuke-lover," "mushroom maniac," or "pepper enthusiast," this recipe is your ticket to savory success. Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized zucchinis, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, zest and juice
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
  • 2 medium-sized zucchinis, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, zest and juice
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your cast iron pan: Place your cast iron pan on the stove over medium-high heat and let it get nice and hot.
  2. Prepare the zucchini: While the pan is heating, thinly slice the zucchinis into rounds. You can use a knife or a mandolin slicer for even slices.
  3. Sear the zucchini: Add the olive oil to the hot cast iron pan and immediately add the sliced zucchinis. Spread them out in a single layer to ensure even cooking. Let them sear for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until they are tender and have a nice golden brown color.
  4. Add garlic and red pepper: About a minute before the zucchinis are done, add the minced garlic and crushed red pepper flakes to the pan. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
  5. Add lemon and mint: Remove the pan from heat and drizzle the lemon juice over the zucchini. Sprinkle the lemon zest and chopped fresh mint leaves on top.
  6. Season and garnish: Season the zucchini with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. If desired, sprinkle with freshly chopped parsley for added flavor and color.
  7. Serve: Transfer the seared zucchini with garlic, lemon, crushed red pepper, and mint leaves to a serving platter, and drizzle any remaining lemony-garlic juices from the pan on top.
  8. Enjoy: Serve this delightful zucchini dish as a side or pair it with your favorite protein for a complete meal. The combination of flavors, from the zesty lemon to the spicy crushed red pepper and the refreshing mint leaves, will tantalize your taste buds. Enjoy!
